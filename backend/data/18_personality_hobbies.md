# Personality, Hobbies & Mindset

## How my friends would describe me

**Innovative.** I tend to come up with unconventional solutions to problems, even in the middle of hackathons under time pressure. I genuinely believe absolute, complete knowledge of a domain can limit your ability to brainstorm — but partial knowledge and thinking a little "out of the blue" opens up new angles. And with today's tech landscape, almost any offbeat idea is implementable in some form if you're willing to figure it out.

**Quick to grasp new concepts.** I pick up new technologies, frameworks, and ideas fast — which is a big part of why I default to "build first, deep-dive later" when learning something new.

**Inclusive.** I make sure everyone around me gets a fair chance to be part of the conversation and doesn't feel left out — whether that's in a hackathon team, a class project, or a casual discussion. I also genuinely respect other people's opinions and perspectives, even when they differ from mine.

## Hobbies & interests

- **Art & sketching** — I've been drawing since childhood, well before I ever wrote code. I keep a personal archive of my old artwork — happy to show it if you're curious, just ask!
- **Painting** — currently looking to transition into learning painting properly, especially experimenting with acrylics.
- **Badminton** — I play a lot, and I'm genuinely good at it.
- **Morning runs** — recently started 5:30 AM walks/runs to stay fit. The actual running is easier than the waking up — but I treat that as a discipline problem, not a motivation problem, and that mindset shift has made all the difference.

## Why the non-technical stuff matters to me

Art and badminton both demand a mix of creativity and precision — the same combination I look for in good engineering. I think staying creative outside of code is exactly what helps me stay detail-oriented and inventive inside it.

## Fun facts

- My sketchbooks are older than my GitHub commits — I've been drawing since I could hold a pencil, way before I wrote my first line of code.
- I'm someone who'll deliberately learn a technology halfway, build something with it, then go back and learn it properly — partial knowledge sparks weirder, more creative ideas than mastering it first.
- Waking up for a 5:30 AM run is genuinely harder for me than debugging a broken pipeline at 1 AM. But that's exactly the point — mindset is what makes the difference, not motivation.

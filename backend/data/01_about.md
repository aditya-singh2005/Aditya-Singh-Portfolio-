# About Me

Hi, I'm Aditya Singh 👋

I'm a Computer Science undergrad at Delhi Technological University (DTU), batch of 2023–2027, currently in my third year. I'm an aspiring Software Engineer with a strong pull toward building scalable, real-world software — and increasingly, toward modern AI systems.

## Why I chose Computer Science

I've been drawn to problem solving and coding for a long time — that fascination is really why I chose this field in the first place. What keeps me going, even now, is the specific feeling of building something and watching it actually work right in front of me, or the moment something new finally clicks while I'm learning it. That feeling is honestly a big part of why I keep taking on new projects and hackathons instead of coasting on what I already know.

## How I like to work

I do my best work with a mix of structure and ambiguity — I like having a clear goal or problem to solve, but I don't need every step mapped out in advance; I'd rather have room to explore and iterate as I build. I'm comfortable with both async work and pairing/collaborating directly with a team — in fact I actively prefer working with others when the problem is complex, since it exposes me to perspectives I wouldn't land on alone. Given a choice, I lean toward environments that give me ownership over a problem rather than a rigid step-by-step spec, since that's where I tend to come up with the most creative solutions.

## What I care about

I don't really enjoy following tutorials just to follow them. Whenever I pick up a new technology, my first instinct is to go build something real with it — that's how I actually learn what it's good for and where its trade-offs bite. I'd rather ship something imperfect and learn from it than sit and "master" something in theory first.

Actually, I lean into this pretty deliberately: I'll often learn a technology just halfway, build something with it, and only go back to learn it properly afterward. I've found that partial knowledge sparks weirder, more creative ideas than mastering something first — absolute knowledge can box you into "the standard way" of doing things, while thinking a little out of the blue tends to produce more innovative solutions. And with the kind of tech available today, almost any idea is implementable in one form or another if you're willing to figure it out.

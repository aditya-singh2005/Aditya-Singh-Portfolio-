# Career Goals & What I'm Looking For

## Current focus

I'm currently looking for **full-time Software Engineering roles**, with a strong interest in roles that also touch AI/ML — I want to keep building strong software engineering fundamentals while staying closely plugged into how fast AI tooling is evolving (RAG, agentic systems, LLM applications), rather than treating AI as a separate track from "real" engineering.

## What I'm looking for in a company

I'm drawn to product-based companies that build and ship their own technology — places where engineering quality genuinely matters to the product outcome, not just internal tooling or pure service work. I care about working somewhere that takes engineering seriously and pays well for that seriousness.

If I had to name a dream company, it'd be **Google** — the scale of engineering problems there, and the caliber of systems they build, is exactly the kind of environment I want to grow in.

## Location & flexibility

I'm flexible when it comes to location and open to relocating for the right opportunity. I generally prefer to keep compensation details for a direct conversation rather than get into specifics here — happy to discuss that through the contact form.

## Long-term direction

I want to work on products involving scalable systems, intelligent applications, and developer infrastructure — combining solid backend/systems engineering with the kind of AI-native thinking that's becoming central to how software gets built.

## Why someone should hire me

A few things I'd point to:

- **A genuine track record of shipping, not just learning** — my proudest examples are Mysterio (a two-phase AI candidate-ranking pipeline built under a strict compute budget) and Looply (a full role-based, audit-logged goal-tracking portal built to real business-rule standards), alongside other production-style full-stack apps (Connectify, FlowDash) and a self-initiated startup-style project (Connecti) with a real business model behind it.
- **Strong CS fundamentals** — DSA, OS, DBMS, computer networks, distributed systems — backed by 750+ solved problems and a peak competitive rating around 1750–1775.
- **A fast, hands-on learning style** — I pick up new tech by building with it immediately, which means I ramp up quickly on unfamiliar stacks rather than needing a long runway.
- **Breadth across the stack** — frontend, backend, real-time systems, databases, and ML/AI, so I can contribute across a product rather than being boxed into one layer.
- **Genuine care about staying current** — I'm actively learning RAG, LangChain, and agentic AI right now because I think that's where software engineering is heading, not because it's trendy.
- **Good collaborator** — I actively value inclusive, respectful teamwork and believe diverse perspectives make the end product better, which shows in how I approach hackathon teams and group projects.

I try to stay flexible and adaptable in general — new tools, new problem domains, new team structures — because I think that adaptability, paired with solid fundamentals, is what actually lets someone grow fast in a role.

## How I like to work

I do my best work with a mix of structure and ambiguity — a clear goal or problem, but room to explore rather than a rigid step-by-step spec. I'm comfortable with both async work and direct pairing/collaboration, and I genuinely prefer working with others on complex problems since it pushes me toward ideas I wouldn't land on alone.
